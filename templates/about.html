I was born in Manila, Philippines on October 2nd, 2002; although, my family is originally from the city of Masbate. I moved to New York City when I was two years old because my mom was offered a job as a high school math teacher there. I grew up in Canarsie, Brooklyn and went through the NYC public school system until I joined Prep for Prep in middle school. Because of this program, I was able to get a scholarship to attend the Browning School on the Upper East Side in Manhattan. Here, I was able to begin exploring my STEM passions. In eleventh grade, I joined the IAC fellowship and began with an internship in front-end development. High school was also an opportunity for me to learn about more aspects of my identity and the world. I attended the Student Diversity Leadership Conference where I learned how to strengthen my character with my diverse indentities. I also participated in the Student Diplomacy Leadership Corps program where I got to spend four weeks in China learning about Chinese culture, Climate Change, and what Chinese scientists have been doing to combat it. These experiences greatly influenced where I ended up going to college: Harvey Mudd, a STEM-focused liberal arts college. As an engineering major, I've been learning under HMC's world-renowned curriculum. Being an "all-around" engineer has helped me succeed in my A.I. Research Internships with J.P. Morgan. Still wanting to learn about the different perspectives of the world, I found myself studying for a semester in Spain at the Uniersidad Carlos III de Madrid. Now, I am a senior and looking forward to what the future has in store for me.
